The Arnprior Town Council voted late Tuesday evening to approve a multi-million dollar budget increase for the upcoming Downtown Revitalization Project. The initiative, which has been in the planning phases for over eighteen months, aims to completely overhaul the infrastructure along John Street, modernizing underground water mains while significantly improving the surface-level streetscape. Municipal planners emphasize that the upgrades are vital for accommodating the town's recent population growth.

Local business owners turned out in large numbers at the town hall meeting to voice both support and concern. While the majority of merchants agree that the aging sidewalks and outdated street lighting require immediate attention, many expressed anxiety regarding potential road closures during the peak summer shopping season. Mayor Lisa Miller assured attendees that construction schedules would be strictly managed to minimize disruptions to foot traffic and retail accessibility.

In addition to core infrastructure repairs, the revitalization plan introduces expanded pedestrian walkways, designated bicycle lanes, and the installation of energy-efficient LED decorative streetlamps. Additional public seating and green spaces will be added near the library intersection, transforming the core into a more inviting space for community gatherings. Funding for the project will be heavily supported by provincial infrastructure grants, reducing the immediate tax burden on residents.

Engineers are expected to finalize the phased construction schedule by the end of this month, with initial breaking of ground slated for early July. Regular town hall updates and a dedicated online portal will be launched next week to keep citizens informed of daily traffic detours and project milestones. Councilors expressed optimism that the short-term disruption will yield long-term economic prosperity for the heart of Arnprior.
