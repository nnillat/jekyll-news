It was a night that will go down in local sports history. The Arnprior Rivermen punched their ticket to the Valley Senior Hockey League Championship series following a breathtaking 4-3 double-overtime victory against their fierce rivals, the Renfrew Timberwolves. A packed house at the Nick Smith Centre watched as local winger Liam Tremblay scored the decisive goal just three minutes into the second sudden-death period, triggering an eruption of cheers from the hometown crowd.

The game was a defensive battle from the opening puck drop. The Timberwolves established an early lead in the first period, capitalizing on two quick power-play opportunities. However, the Rivermen displayed remarkable resilience, battling back in the second frame with goals from defenseman Cole Harrison and veteran forward Ryan Campbell. The third period saw relentless back-and-forth action, ultimately ending in a 3-3 deadlock that set the stage for the dramatic overtime finish.

Rivermen goaltender Jacob Mercer was named the first star of the match, turning away a staggering forty-two shots, including a crucial breakaway save late in the first overtime period that kept his team's championship dreams alive. Head coach Dan Tremblay praised his squad's grit and determination, noting that the team's rigorous conditioning and disciplined defensive structure over the last several months paid off when it mattered most.

The best-of-seven championship series against the Carleton Place Kings is scheduled to kick off this coming Thursday night on foreign ice, with Game Two returning to Arnprior on Saturday evening. Team management expects tickets for the home game to sell out rapidly, prompting plans to broadcast the away matches live on big screens inside the community hall for fans unable to travel.
