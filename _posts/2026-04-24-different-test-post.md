The shores of Robert Simpson Park will come alive with vibrant colors and rhythmic drumming this weekend as the annual Arnprior Dragon Boat Festival makes its triumphant return. Organizers confirmed that over thirty corporate, community, and competitive teams have officially registered to race down the Ottawa River. The event, which raises vital funds for regional healthcare initiatives, marks its fourteenth year as a staple of the local summer calendar.

Spectators can look forward to more than just high-stakes water races. The festival committee has significantly expanded the shoreline attractions this year, introducing a dedicated local artisan market, a craft beer garden featuring regional breweries, and a lineup of live musical performances showcasing Ottawa Valley talent. Activities tailored for children, including face painting and interactive educational exhibits by local conservation authorities, will run continuously throughout the weekend.

The competitive brackets are looking particularly fierce this season, with defending champions "The Valley Vipers" returning to protect their title against a surge of new corporate entries from local manufacturing plants and small businesses. Funds raised through team registrations and community donations during the event will be directed entirely toward the purchase of new diagnostic equipment for the Arnprior Regional Health center, directly benefiting local families.

Volunteers are still being sought to assist with parking coordination, shoreline safety, and eco-station management. With clear skies and warm temperatures forecasted for Saturday, town officials are advising attendees to utilize the complimentary shuttle buses operating from the Nick Smith Centre parking lot to reduce traffic congestion near the park waterfront.
