A cornerstone of the local culinary scene is entering an exciting new chapter. The Historic Elgin Street Bakery announced yesterday a major commercial expansion that will double its daily production capacity. The family-owned enterprise, which has served traditional pastries, artisanal sourdough, and custom cakes to generations of residents, has acquired the adjacent retail unit to accommodate a state-of-the-art commercial kitchen facility.

The expansion comes as a direct response to a massive surge in demand from both local storefront patrons and regional wholesale accounts, including boutique grocery stores across the Ottawa Valley. By expanding their footprint, the bakery will now be able to introduce a gluten-free pastry line and an expanded breakfast menu featuring locally sourced ingredients. The move is also a win for employment, resulting in the immediate creation of five new full-time positions.

Head baker and co-owner Elena Dubois noted that while the technology in the kitchen is being modernized, the core philosophy of the business remains entirely unchanged. Every loaf of bread will continue to follow the slow-fermentation methods that earned the shop its loyal following. The expansion also includes a renovated front-of-house area, offering increased indoor seating and an aesthetic that pays homage to Arnprior’s rich industrial and lumber milling history.

A grand reopening celebration is officially scheduled for next Friday, featuring free pastry samples, coffee tastings in partnership with Madawaska roasters, and a ribbon-cutting ceremony attended by members of the Greater Arnprior Chamber of Commerce. Dubois expressed immense gratitude to the community for their unwavering support throughout the transition period.
